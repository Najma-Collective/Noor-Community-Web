Our Educational Programmes

Each programme provides an academically rigorous and affirming experience, equipping learners with sophisticated linguistic and analytical tools.

Our curriculum is founded on collaborative, problem-posing methodologies that cultivate creativity and criticality, fostering individual agency within a supportive community of practice.

General and Business English

Task-Based

Language is acquired through solving tangible, real-world problems

Our courses utilise Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT), where learners engage with dynamic, collaborative challenges. We've developed robust scaffolding with interactive chatbot-based micro-lessons to ensure classroom time is dedicated to meaningful engagement.

Example Scenarios:

  • • Palestinian arts collective negotiating global exhibitions
  • • Tech start-up pitching to international investors
  • • Social enterprise developing sustainable business plans
  • • Media team creating content for global audiences

Speculative Practice & Worldbuilding

Project-based modules incorporate elements of live-action roleplay and worldbuilding. Students inhabit persistent professional roles—managing a record label, running an architectural firm—for several weeks. This sustained methodology allows learners to project and perform possible futures, expanding their 'capacity to aspire' in contexts marked by foreclosure.

Writing Programme

Genre-Based

Develop conscious control over written communication

Integrated within our Business English curriculum, the writing programme employs a genre-based pedagogy. Understanding the social purpose and conventional structures of a text is key to achieving communicative goals.

Professional Genres

CVs • Business Plans • Financial Statements • Persuasive Emails • Reports • Proposals

English for Academic Purposes

Portfolio

Linguistic and analytical competencies for higher education success

Our EAP programme provides students with the specific competencies required for university success. The curriculum is systematically structured around the Oxford EAP series, offering a robust framework for developing critical thinking, academic argumentation, and scholarly engagement.

Portfolio-Based Assessment

Students curate their work, reflect on progress, and set future learning goals—promoting the 'mature learner autonomy' central to our pedagogical vision.

Mentoring Programme

3 Months

A practice of accompaniment grounded in solidarity

A three-month structured experience guiding students in planning and executing self-directed projects. Each project has a concrete, practical outcome—from creating professional portfolios to developing coding projects.

The Process

  1. Define a well-scoped, personally significant project
  2. Get paired with an expert mentor in your field
  3. Work together through challenges and milestones
  4. Complete your project and build your capabilities

The mentor-mentee relationship is a partnership designed to support the student's own vision, enhancing their confidence to pursue lives they have reason to value.
